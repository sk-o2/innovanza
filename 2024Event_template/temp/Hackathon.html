HACKATHON

What it’s all about.

Theme- "Think. Code. Solve.: The hackathon drives tech-powered solutions with creativity, efficiency, and expertise. "

Welcome to Hackathon 2025 – where innovation meets ingenuity! This electrifying offline tech showdown pushes your coding and problem-solving prowess to the absolute limit. Compete in teams to crack real-world challenges, craft groundbreaking solutions, and showcase your brilliance in a high-stakes, adrenaline-fueled coding marathon. Whether you're a passionate coder, an aspiring developer, or a seasoned programmer, this is your golden opportunity to shine, battle it out with the sharpest minds, and turn visionary ideas into reality. Ready to make your mark? The future is yours to code!

Roadmap

Flow of Event

Registration Phase
Participants register and form teams.
Release of Problem Statement
Problem statements will be shared at the beginning of the event.
Development Phase
Team work on their projects within the given time frame.
Round 1: Online Submission (PPT Presentation)
  • Problem Statement – The challenge being addressed.
  • Proposed Solution – Overview of the idea and approach.
  • Technology Stack – Tools, frameworks, and languages used.
  • Innovation & Impact – Uniqueness and real-world application.
  • Feasibility & Execution – Implementation plan and scalability.
Offline Development & Pitching
Shortlisted teams will participate in an offline round where they will build their solution and present it to the judges. Teams will work on transforming their idea into a functional prototype.Each team will showcase their solution, demonstrating its features, and feasibility.
Submission
Participants submit their solutions before the deadline
Evaluation
Judges review the projects based on predefined criteria.

Rules and Regulations

1. Team must consist of 3-5 members.

2. Solutions must be built within the event duration using any tech stack.

3. No plagiarism or pre-existing projects.

4. Teams can be formed before or during the event.

5. Maintain professionalism; misconduct leads to disqualification.

6. Rule violations result in immediate disqualification. Any form of malpractice or unethical behavior will result in immediate disqualification.

8. Participants must be available for Q&A sessions if required by the judges. Judges' and organizers' decisions are final.

Judging Criteria

1.Innovation & Creativity –+How unique and innovative is the solution?

2.Functionality –How well does the solution work?

3.Functionality – The level of technical expertise demonstrated.

4.User Experience (UX/UI) –Design, ease of use, and effectiveness.

5.Presentation & Documentation –Clarity of explanation and completeness of submission.
